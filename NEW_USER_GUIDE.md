# Sportz-Well Marketing Studio
## New User Guide — First Time Setup and Use
### Plain English. No jargon. Step by step.

---

## What Is This App?

This app is your AI-powered social media content factory for SWPI.

You give it a topic. It searches the web for relevant articles, turns them into post ideas,
writes the actual Instagram and Facebook posts, checks them for quality, and helps you plan
when to publish them.

**You are always in control.** The app never posts anything by itself. You review and
approve every single step before anything goes live.

---

## What You Need Before Starting

1. A computer with internet access
2. The app URL: **https://swpi-marketing.streamlit.app**
3. That's it. No installation. No downloads. Open it in any browser.

## Part 1 — First Time Setup (do this once only)

### Step 1 — Open the app

Go to: **https://swpi-marketing.streamlit.app**

You will see the Sportz-Well Marketing Studio home page with a dark background
and gold text.

### Step 2 — Set up the brand data

The app needs to know about SWPI before it can write anything.

1. Click **Brand Brain** in the left sidebar
2. Click the **Seed Sportz-Well + SWPI** button (top right, red button)
3. Tick the checkbox that says "I understand this will overwrite existing data"
4. Click **Seed Sportz-Well + SWPI (overwrites existing)**
5. Wait 5 seconds
6. Click **Overview** tab — you should see SWPI's full profile loaded

**You only do this once.** After this the brand data is saved permanently.

### Step 3 — Confirm the home page looks right

Click **app** in the left sidebar to go back to the home page.

You should see:
- SWPI listed as the active client
- Phase 1 — Grassroots Cricket as the active phase
- All 8 modules showing ✓ Ready

If you see this, setup is complete. You are ready to use the app.

## Part 2 — Your First Content Batch (do this every week)

This is the full workflow from zero to scheduled posts. Follow these steps in order.

---

### STEP 1 — Research (Monday, ~10 minutes, costs ~$0.60)

**What it does:** Searches the web for relevant articles about a topic you choose.
These articles become the raw material for your posts.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Research** in the left sidebar
2. You are on Tab A (Run Research) by default
3. In the **Topic to research** box, type a specific topic. Examples:
   - `grassroots cricket academy development India 2026`
   - `coach parent communication youth cricket`
   - `mental toughness training young cricketers`
   - `cricket selection trials under-16 Mumbai`
4. Leave **Geography** on "Indian-heavy (3:2)"
5. Leave **Max items** on 6
6. Leave **Min relevance** on 7
7. Click the red **Run Research** button
8. Wait 30–60 seconds
9. You will see: "Saved X items · Cost: $X.XX"

**Important:** Type a subject, not a website URL. Wrong: `https://www.sportz-well.com`.
Right: `grassroots cricket academy India`.

**After running:** Click **Tab B (Research Library)** to see what was found.
Delete any items that are clearly not relevant (click the item to expand it,
then click Delete).

---

### STEP 2 — Strategy (~5 minutes to run, then 10 minutes to review, costs ~$0.10)

**What it does:** Takes all your saved research and turns it into post ideas
called "story angles." Each angle is a post topic with a hook, a brief,
and tags for platform and format.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Strategy** in the left sidebar
2. You are on Tab A (Run Strategist) by default
3. Check the green box — it should say how many research items are available
4. Leave sliders at default (7 relevance, 12 angles)
5. Leave Editorial focus empty (or type something like "focus on parent communication")
6. Click the red **Propose Story Angles** button
7. Wait 30–60 seconds
8. You will see: "Proposed X angles across X themes · Cost: $X.XX"

**If you get an error:** Just click the button again. This step occasionally fails
and always works on the second try.

**After running — review and approve angles:**

1. Click **Tab B (Story Angles Library)**
2. You will see angles grouped by theme
3. Read each angle — the title, description, and editorial brief
4. For angles you like: click the green **Approve** button
5. For angles you don't want: click **Reject**
6. Aim to approve 4–6 angles per week
7. Only approved angles get turned into posts

---

### STEP 3 — Generate Drafts (~5 minutes, costs ~$0.08 per angle)

**What it does:** Takes your approved angles and writes the actual Instagram
and Facebook posts. It writes 2 different versions of each post (called Variant 1
and Variant 2) so you can pick the one you prefer.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Drafts** in the left sidebar
2. You are on Tab 1 (Generate Drafts) by default
3. You will see how many approved angles are waiting
4. Click **Generate ALL Approved Angles**
5. Wait 1–3 minutes (each angle takes about 15–20 seconds)
6. You will see: "Created X drafts across X angles · Cost: $X.XX"

**After generating:** Move to the next step before approving anything.
Always run the Editor first.

---

### STEP 4 — Editor Review (~10 minutes, costs ~$0.027 per draft)

**What it does:** An AI quality checker that reads each draft and flags problems.
It checks for banned words, wrong URL format, posts that are too long or too short,
and other brand guardrail violations. It never rewrites — it just tells you what
is wrong.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Editor** in the left sidebar
2. You are on Tab 1 (Review Drafts) by default
3. Click **Review All Unreviewed Drafts**
4. Watch the progress bar — it reviews one draft at a time
5. When done you will see: "Reviewed X drafts · Clean: X · Flagged: X"

**After reviewing:**

1. Click **Tab 2 (Editor Library)**
2. Green = Clean. No problems. Safe to approve.
3. Red flag = Flagged. Has issues. Click to expand and read them.

**Common issues and what to do:**

| Issue | What it means | What to do |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| CAPTION_TOO_SHORT | Post is a few words under the minimum | Edit the draft and add one sentence |
| CAPTION_TOO_LONG | Post is a few words over the maximum | Edit and cut one sentence |
| HYPE_WORD | Used a banned word like "game-changing" | Edit and remove the word |
| URL_FORMAT | Link has www. in front | Edit and remove the www. |
| GENERIC_HOOK | Opening line is too vague | Edit the first sentence |

**To edit a draft:**
1. Go to **Drafts** in the sidebar
2. Click **Tab 2 (Drafts Library)**
3. Find the draft → click **Edit**
4. Make your changes → click Save
5. Go back to Editor → click **Re-review** on that draft

**A flagged draft is not automatically rejected.** Minor issues (2 words too short)
can be ignored and the draft approved anyway. Use your judgment.

---

### STEP 5 — Approve Your Drafts (~5 minutes)

**What it does:** You decide which drafts are good enough to publish.
Only approved drafts can be scheduled.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Drafts** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Tab 2 (Drafts Library)**
3. Read each draft — headline, body, CTA, and hashtags
4. For each draft you want to use: click **Approve**
5. For drafts you don't want: click **Reject**
6. You will usually have 2 variants per angle — approve the one you prefer,
   or approve both if both are good

---

### STEP 6 — Media Briefs (~5 minutes, costs ~$0.018 per brief)

**What it does:** Takes each approved draft's image description and turns it into
a detailed photography brief for your content team — telling them exactly what to
shoot, where, what props to use, and what to avoid.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Media** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Tab 1 (Generate)**
3. Click **Generate All Pending Briefs**
4. Wait ~10 seconds per draft
5. Click **Tab 2 (Library)**
6. Read each brief → click **Approve** if it looks right

**Give approved briefs to your photographer or content team** before the shoot day.
Each brief tells them: shot type, subject, setting, lighting, props, colours,
what to avoid, and how the visual should match the post's hook.

---

### STEP 7 — Schedule Posts (~10 minutes, free)

**What it does:** Assigns each approved draft to a specific date and time on your
content calendar.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Calendar** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Tab 1 (Schedule a Draft)**
3. Pick a draft from the dropdown
4. Pick the date you want to post it
5. Pick the time (recommended: 9:00 AM or 6:00 PM for Indian audience)
6. Click **Confirm Schedule**
7. Repeat for each approved draft

**Recommended posting schedule for SWPI:**
- 3 posts per week per platform (Instagram + Facebook)
- Space them out: Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- Time: 9 AM or 6 PM IST

---

### STEP 8 — Publish to Meta Business Suite (ongoing)

**What it does:** You copy the final post from this app and paste it into
Meta Business Suite to actually publish it on Instagram and Facebook.

**How to do it:**

1. Click **Calendar** → **Tab 2 (Calendar View)**
2. On the scheduled day, find the post
3. Click on it to see the full text
4. Copy the body text, hashtags, and CTA
5. Open Meta Business Suite (business.facebook.com)
6. Create a new post, paste the text, add the photo from your shoot
7. Schedule or publish
8. Come back to this app → Calendar → click **Mark Posted**

**Always click Mark Posted** after publishing. It keeps your pipeline accurate
and the home page status updated.

## Troubleshooting — Common Problems

**"No client set up yet"**
Go to Brand Brain → Seed Sportz-Well + SWPI. This runs the first-time setup.

**"No approved, unscheduled drafts" on Calendar page**
Your drafts are not approved yet. Go to Drafts → approve some drafts first.

**Strategy failed with a JSON error**
This happens occasionally. Click the button again — it always works on retry.

**Draft is flagged for CAPTION_TOO_SHORT (e.g. 148 words, needs 150)**
Edit the draft and add one sentence. Or approve it anyway — 2 words short is not
a real problem.

**The app is slow to load**
The app sleeps when not used. The first visit of the day takes 15–30 seconds to
wake up. This is normal — just wait and refresh.

**I accidentally ran Strategy twice and got duplicate angles**
Go to Strategy → Story Angles Library → reject the duplicates. They will not
be drafted.

## Important Rules — Never Do These

- Never type a website URL as a research topic (e.g. `https://www.sportz-well.com`)
- Never skip the Editor step before approving drafts
- Never change the Brand Brain profile without understanding the impact — it
  affects every future post the AI generates
- Never approve a draft you would not actually publish
- Never forget to click Mark Posted after publishing — it keeps the pipeline accurate

## Glossary
